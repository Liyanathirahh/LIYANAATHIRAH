Let's Go!

The lessons I've learnt while working as a kindergarten teacher at a
school near my home will be the focus of this post. All of the
children are quite active, and they learn best when they are able to
put their knowledge into practise. When it comes to participating in
the discussion, they are a rambunctious bunch that constantly via
for attention. Besides the girls, I have a few energetic boys. My
students can often keep their cool for 20-30 minutes at a time.
However, I have 5 young guys who can't keep their cool for even
5 minutes at a time. Rather than constantly involving my students
in their own learning, I find it difficult to "feed" them content.
My students' abilities span from excellent to terrible.
In contrast, the kids who do the worst in my class know only a few
letters of the alphabet, fail to build meaningful sentences, and
usually react to questions with claims that have nothing to do with
the issue at hand. The most difficult component of developing a lesson
plan for this period of instruction was finding a way to actively include
all of my students and offer them with opportunity for success regardless
of their various levels of skill.
